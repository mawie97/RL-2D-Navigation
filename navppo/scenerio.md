| **Level** | **Environment Setup & Variation**                                     | **Special Features**                                            | **Training Focus / Purpose**                                                                                 |
| --------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Level 1   | Basic open area with no obstacles.                                    | No obstacles present; direct line to goal.                      | Learn basic movement and goal-reaching without obstructions.                                                 |
| Level 2   | Open area with a single static obstacle.                              | One obstacle introduces simple avoidance challenge.             | Introduce obstacle avoidance and path adjustment around an object.                                           |
| Level 3   | Open area with multiple static obstacles (3–5).                       | Increased obstacle count creates moderate complexity.           | Develop navigation strategies around several obstacles; basic path planning.                                 |
| Level 4   | Larger area with many obstacles (5–10) and a longer path to the goal. | Higher obstacle density and extended route length.              | Practice long-range navigation and sustained obstacle avoidance over a longer episode.                       |
| Level 5   | Maze-like environment with corridors and potential dead-ends.         | Corridors constrain movement; some paths may lead to dead ends. | Train maze navigation skills, including decision-making and backtracking when encountering dead-ends.        |
| Level 6   | Fully randomized environment combining elements from previous levels. | Mixed layouts and obstacle placements vary each episode.        | Test generalization and adaptability to unpredictable, complex scenarios incorporating all prior challenges. |


| **Level**                                | **Environment Setup & Variation**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                | **Special Features**                                                                                                                                                                                        | **Training Focus / Purpose**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| ---------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Level 1: Basic**     | **Obstacles:** 0 (none).<br>**Agent-Goal Distance:** Varied (short to long).<br>**Agent Start:** Random position (goal placed randomly).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         | Open field with no obstacle.                                                                                                                                     | Learn basic movement and goal-reaching                                                                                                                         |
| **Level 2: Single Obstacle**     | **Obstacles:** 1 static obstacle.<br>**Agent-Goal Distance:** Varied.<br>**Obstacle Placement:** Varies in distance from agent (start from far to close to give agent some time to react at first then closer to test reaction).                                                                                                                                                                                                | A single obstacle is placed roughly between the agent and goal (not directly at start, to avoid immediate collision). The obstacle might sometimes block the direct path, forcing slight detour.            | *Introductory obstacle avoidance.* The agent practices detecting one obstacle and navigating around it while still reaching the goal. This teaches **basic avoidance maneuvers** without overwhelming complexity.                                                                                                                                            |
| **Level 3: Multiple Obstacles**          | **Obstacles:** 3–5 static obstacles.<br>**Agent-Goal Distance:** Varied (short distances use ~3 obstacles; longer distances use up to 5).<br>**Obstacle Placement:** Obstacles positioned along the direct route between agent and goal (ensuring the straight-line path is blocked).                                                                                                                            | Moderately cluttered environment. The agent’s direct line-of-sight to the goal is often *blocked*, requiring path planning around obstacles. Obstacles may be spaced out to allow multiple possible paths.  | *Intermediate navigation and path planning.* The agent learns to handle **several obstacles** in the environment, planning a path through a moderately cluttered space. This level builds the agent’s ability to detour and make navigation decisions when the straightforward path is not free.                                                             |
| **Level 4: High-Density Obstacles**      | **Obstacles:** 5–10 static obstacles (higher count if environment size allows).<br>**Agent-Goal Distance:** Long range (the goal is placed far from the agent, requiring longer navigation).<br>**Obstacle Placement:** Spread across the area, with some obstacles likely **on the path** to the goal.                                                                                                          | A heavily cluttered open area. Obstacle **density** is higher, so the agent must navigate a complex, potentially winding route. (Still generally open-area, *not* a narrow corridor yet.)                   | *Advanced obstacle avoidance.* The agent practices **robust navigation** in a highly cluttered environment. By encountering many obstacles over a long path, the agent improves in maintaining goal-direction while avoiding frequent obstacles.                                                                                                             |
| **Level 5: Narrow Passages & Dead-Ends** | **Obstacles:** Variable (could reuse 3–10 obstacles, but arranged as walls/corridors).<br>**Agent-Goal Distance:** Medium to long, sometimes requiring entering or exiting a confined area.<br>**Placement:** Environment includes **corridors or dead-end** sections formed by obstacles (e.g. U-shaped dead-end or narrow hallway). The agent or the goal is placed inside these structures in some scenarios. | Constrained spaces introduced. For example, a corridor only slightly wider than the agent, or a cul-de-sac where the goal is hidden behind obstacles (no direct line-of-sight until entering the dead-end). | *Corner-case handling and precision.* The agent learns to **navigate tight spaces** and avoid getting stuck. It may need to backtrack out of a dead-end or carefully move through a hallway. This trains more **precise control** and the ability to handle scenarios where the optimal path is not obvious (e.g. going around a blockage to find the goal). |
| **Level 6: Mixed / Comprehensive**       | **Obstacles:** Mixed (0 up to 10, randomly chosen).<br>**Agent-Goal Distance:** Mixed (both short and long instances).<br>**Environment Structure:** Mixed — a random combination of open fields, cluttered fields, and occasional corridor/dead-end setups.                                                                                                                                                     | **Diverse scenarios** presented randomly. One episode might be a simple open field, the next a narrow corridor, etc. This ensures the agent cannot overfit to one type of scenario.                         | *Full generalization training.* By **randomizing all the above factors**, this final stage exposes the agent to a wide variety of situations. The goal is to ensure the agent can generalize and handle **any** arrangement of obstacles and distances (similar to a random scenario generator, but after it has built up skills from levels 1–5).           |
